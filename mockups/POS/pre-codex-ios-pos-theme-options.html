Pick rubric — how to decide

  • Must not compete with cream. Any hue too close to beige / yellow / orange makes cream invisible. Options A / G / H pass cleanly.
  • Must not collide with semantic colors. Option C (forest green) conflicts with success. Option D (espresso) borders on warning amber at high-saturation areas.
  • Atmospheric vs. flat. Options A / E / F are flat (no radials). Options G / H keep the ambient glow without purple. Flat = more Apple-like minimalism; radial = more boutique / premium-feel.
  • Shop identity. If the business is tech-repair-first = slate family (B / H). If it's retail-cream-bizarre brand = warm family (A / G / D). If minimal / modern = OLED or cream-dark (E / F).
Shortest answer: pick G · Warm graphite + cream radial. It drops the purple (your complaint) while preserving the moody ambient feel that makes cream glow. Flat graphite (A) loses the atmosphere; slate (B) shifts the whole product identity.
