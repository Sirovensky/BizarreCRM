BIZARRE CRM

Design System

One unified palette across web, iOS, and Android. Two modes — dark and light — sharing the same warm-graphite surface ramp. Brand cream stays the constant; the accent shifts to caramel in light mode for AA contrast. Same identity, every device.

★ Option F 11-stop surface ramp Cream / Caramel pair WCAG AA

Principle

⚖️

Restraint

Cream is the brand. So it appears sparingly — small CTAs, 2px hero edges, step dots, single-letter avatars. Never as a wash. Restraint makes the cream pop when it does land.

🌡️

Warm neutrals

Surfaces are warm-graphite — between stone and neutral. Avoids the cold blue-gray of generic SaaS, avoids the over-warm beige that reads as "accidental." Reads premium, not retail.

♻️

One ramp, two modes

Same 11-stop surface ramp drives both modes. Slot mapping shifts: in dark, page = surface-900; in light, page = surface-50. Components written once read correctly in either mode.

Primary — Brand

Cream is the brand identity. It anchors dark mode. Light mode shifts to caramel — same hue family, darker stop — because cream on white fails AA at 1.1:1. Caramel passes at 5.2:1. Same brand, mode-appropriate weight.

Do / Don't

✓ DO
  • Cream as edge, not wash. 2px left border on dark hero cards; small CTAs; step dots.
  • Caramel in light mode. Anywhere cream lives in dark, caramel takes its place in light. Same role.
  • Surface ramp = neutral. Wide canvases (page, sidebar, cards) use surface-* tokens, never primary tints.
  • Status colors = hue-shifted. Use #E2526C / #34C47E / #E8A33D — not Tailwind defaults — to harmonize with warm brand.
  • Same component, both modes. Don't fork components per mode. Use mode-aware tokens.
✗ DON'T
  • Cream on white. 1.1:1 contrast — fails WCAG, invisible. Use caramel in light mode.
  • Cream wash on dark. bg-primary-50/30 as a wide panel fill drowns the brand mark. Restrict to small accents.
  • Cool grays. Don't reach for Tailwind zinc-* or slate-* on surfaces — kills the warmth. Use surface-*.
  • Generic Tailwind status colors. red-500, green-500, amber-500 read as flat SaaS. Use the hue-shifted brand status tokens.
  • Burgundy on body content. Tertiary is for VIP / loyalty / marketing — never on regular text or primary CTAs.
