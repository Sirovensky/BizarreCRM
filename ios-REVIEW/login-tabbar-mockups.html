Implementation effort, picking-the-final option
  • A — LinearWavyProgressIndicator: ~80 lines. Replace TabRow with a Row of 3 captions over a LinearWavyProgressIndicator(progress = stepIndex / 3f). Tappable past captions wired to existing goToTab. Pairs visually with the brand wave above the wordmark.
  • B — Segmented button row: ~60 lines. SingleChoiceSegmentedButtonRow with 3 SegmentedButton children. Heaviest visual weight; reads like a settings toggle.
  • C — Pill chip row: ~50 lines. Three FilterChips with FilterChipDefaults.brandColors() already shipped. Reuses existing brand contract; closest to current TabRow but with cleaner selected state.

My read: option A reads as progress through a multi-step flow (which is what this is) better than tabs — and reuses the brand wave language. Option B is the most "M3 Expressive" but feels heavy on a login screen. Option C is the conservative pick — minimum redesign, maximum brand consistency with the rest of the app.
