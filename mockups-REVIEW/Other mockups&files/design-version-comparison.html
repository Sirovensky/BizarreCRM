Material 3 — what each version actually gives you

The Android compile gate surfaced a fact the public docs hid: MaterialExpressiveTheme, MotionScheme, and most expressive surface APIs in material3 1.4.0 stable (Sept 2025) are internal. They were promoted to public only in 1.5.0-alpha18 (April 2026). This page shows what each of the three decision paths actually renders on a phone, so you can pick based on what you see.

1.5.0-alpha18 — override to alpha

✓ What you get

  • MaterialExpressiveTheme with cream palette
  • MotionScheme.expressive() — snappy springs everywhere automatically
  • MaterialShapes morph library (cookie / pentagon / clover / arch / softSquare)
  • LoadingIndicator + ContainedLoadingIndicator (shape-morphing) replacing CircularProgressIndicator in button spinners
  • WavyProgressIndicator for the 6-step check-in progress bar
  • ButtonGroup / ToggleButton / SplitButton segmented controls
  • HorizontalFloatingToolbar + DockedToolbar available for POS cart action rows
  • FilterChip with shape-morphing overloads
  • Modifier.animateBounds + SharedTransitionLayout for hero-to-detail flows

✗ What you pay

  • material3 alpha: API can shift before 1.5.0 stable (historically alpha-to-stable diffs are minor)
  • @OptIn(ExperimentalMaterial3ExpressiveApi::class) required everywhere that uses the new surface
  • Regression risk isolated to material3 only — rest of Compose stays on stable BOM
  • Feature flag USE_EXPRESSIVE_THEME = true in debug; false in release until QA passes

1.4.0 stable, limited

~ What you get

  • Cream palette already shipped — no visual change
  • PullToRefreshBox (already used in 26 screens) gains the 1.4 shape-morph behavior automatically
  • New FilterChip / ElevatedFilterChip overloads (stable but barely-visible differences)
  • Material Symbols recommended over deprecated Material Icons (lint-level nudge)

✗ What you don't get

  • MaterialExpressiveTheme — internal, inaccessible to app code
  • MotionScheme.expressive() — internal
  • LoadingIndicator / WavyProgressIndicator — internal
  • ButtonGroup / SplitButton / ToggleButton — internal
  • FloatingToolbar / DockedToolbar — internal
  • MaterialShapes morph library — internal
  • 99%% of the "Material 3 Expressive" surface stays locked until 1.5.0 stable ships

Effective outcome: visually identical to what we already shipped last wave. Calling this "M3 Expressive adoption" would be marketing, not engineering.

Abort migration

~ What you get

  • Zero visual change — wave 1 cream POS is final until material3 1.5.0 stable
  • Revert Phase 0 BOM bump + DragAndDropSupport.kt tweak (2-file revert)
  • Removes risk of any 1.4 BOM regression, no alpha exposure
  • Frees the next design sprint to plan against a stable target

✗ What you leave on the table

  • The POS redesign stays on material3 1.3.x forever (until someone re-opens the ticket)
  • No WavyProgressIndicator on the check-in bar
  • No shape-morph on hero surfaces
  • Wave 2 (this M3 audit) was spent learning about the internal-API gotcha

Effective outcome: indistinguishable from today. The cream POS ships as is.

Compare column 1 (alpha) to columns 2/3 (stable / abort) side by side. Columns 2 and 3 are intentionally identical — that is the whole point. Without alpha, you cannot tell the difference between "limited adoption on stable" and "abort the migration entirely" from a phone screenshot.
